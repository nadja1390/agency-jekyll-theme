About

Since 2016 I have been working in several companies related to Agricultural Water Management with the help of data from the ground, UAVs, and satellites. During my PhD I specialized observing waterlogging from space. In agriculture the occurence of waterlogging is mostly a manmade adverse phenomena that can be largely prevented by informed decisions based on data and applying radical new production models that thrive on diversity: Regenerative Agriculture. The last few years I have focussed on the possibility of satellite data to monitor Regenerative Agricultural Practices. Now I am looking forward to continue my journey to help show the hydrological impact of our future agricultural system.

  • 2016

    Hydrology in droughtprone region in India

    MSc thesis and fieldwork on a socio-hydrological framework for smallholder farmer in Marathwada, Maharashtra, India. How capitalism drives the situation of smallholder cotton farmers to an even more precarious situation.

  • 2017

    Small-scale Agriculture in Mozambique

    During my time at Research Consultancy FutureWater I was responsible for the execution of the project Flying Sensors, where UAVs were enabled to assist local farmers in Mozambique in their daily practices.
