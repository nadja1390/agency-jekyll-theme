About

Since 2016, I haveve immersed myself in the dynamic field of Agricultural Water Management, leveraging cutting-edge data from the ground, UAVs, and satellites. My PhD journey honed my expertise in detecting waterlogging from space—a largely manmade issue in agriculture that can be mitigated through informed, data-driven decisions. The key lies in adopting innovative production models rooted in diversity: Regenerative Agriculture. In recent years, I have focused on harnessing satellite data to monitor the transformative potential of Regenerative Agricultural Practices. This work fuels my passion for demonstrating how a forward-thinking agricultural system can create positive hydrological impacts. But talk alone will not cut it —this is the time for ACTION RESEARCH. That is why my family and I have fully embraced this mission, choosing to live as Agroforestry Farmers in Asturias, Spain. Here, we bridge theory and practice, living the very principles we advocate for, as we work toward shaping a resilient (agricultural) future.

  • 2016

    Hydrology in droughtprone region in India

    MSc thesis and fieldwork on a socio-hydrological framework for smallholder farmer in Marathwada, Maharashtra, India. How capitalism drives the situation of smallholder cotton farmers to an even more precarious situation.

  • 2017

    Small-scale Agriculture in Mozambique

    During my time at Research Consultancy FutureWater I was responsible for the execution of the project Flying Sensors, where UAVs were enabled to assist local farmers in Mozambique in their daily agricultural practices.

  • 2018-2022

    Large-scale Agriculture in Mozambique

    During my time at VanderSat I focused on Agricultural applications of Satellite Remote Sensing, in particular for irrigation. I lead a project to advance Water Productivity with the help of data. The project encouraged me to start a PhD in observing waterlogging from space. A pressing issue in agriculture, especially in the sugarcane plantation we studied in Mozambique.
